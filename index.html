Sketch A: Layered Orbit Clock

This clock explores time as an expressive design object using simple shapes and motion instead of numbers. It supports quick “glanceable” reading while keeping the interface playful and memorable.

  • Context: A personal artifact such as a wearable piece, desktop object, or ambient display where visual style matters alongside functionality.
  • Design decisions: Hours are encoded as a 12-slot indicator that fills cumulatively (e.g., 3 PM fills three slots), shifting the hour reading from a moving hand to a discrete “progress” structure. To create a stronger visual identity, the 3/6/9 positions are triangles instead of circles, adding a graphic rhythm while preserving readability. Minutes are shown as a continuous arc to convey flow through the hour, and seconds are an orbiting dot to keep the clock feeling alive. Hover reveals the exact digital time for precision without clutter.
  • Future work: I want to refine color contrast for accessibility and explore a mode that visually distinguishes AM vs. PM without adding numbers.

Sketch B: Chained Hands

This sketch reinterprets the familiar clock hand metaphor by treating time units as physically connected rather than independent. The design emphasizes relationships between hours, minutes, and seconds instead of isolating them.

  • Context: This clock is imagined as a small sculptural object for a desk or wall, where users can appreciate motion and form over time.
  • Design decisions: The minute hand begins at the end of the hour hand, and the second hand begins at the end of the minute hand, forming a chained structure that visually encodes hierarchy and dependency. Visual hierarchy is reinforced using stroke weight and color. Hover interaction highlights each segment and labels its role, supporting understanding without cluttering the display.
  • Future work: Future versions could experiment with smoother motion transitions or subtle trails to emphasize temporal flow.

Sketch C: Accumulated Day Clock

This sketch explores time as both continuous flow and accumulation, representing not only the current moment but also how a day builds up over time.

  • Context: This clock is designed for ambient or desktop use in work or study environments, where users benefit from awareness of both the present time and overall daily progress.
  • Design decisions: Minutes and seconds share a single outer ring, with minutes shown as a progress arc and seconds as a moving dot, reinforcing a unified sense of flow. Hours are encoded as thin concentric rings that accumulate up to 24, making the passage of the day visible. Stroke weight establishes hierarchy, with hours as structure and minute/second as active motion. Hover reveals exact digital time for precision.
  • Future work: Future iterations could visually differentiate AM and PM or extend the design to include a subtle date-based layer.
